Legacy

Dick Wray (1933-2011) was recognized as one of Houston's most significant abstract expressionist painters. His energetic, gestural works were characterized by bold brushstrokes, vibrant colors, and complex layering techniques that captured the energy and chaos of the modern world.

Throughout his career spanning more than five decades, Wray influenced generations of Texas artists and helped establish Houston as an important center for contemporary art. His architectural background informed his unique approach to composition and space within his artwork.

Wray's distinctive style combined elements of abstract expressionism with his own unique vision, creating works that were both deeply personal and universally resonant. Critics often noted his ability to balance chaos and control in his compositions, creating dynamic tensions that activate the canvas.
